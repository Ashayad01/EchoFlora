Plant Care
Guide

Light Requirements

Most houseplants thrive in bright, indirect light. Place your plant near a window with filtered sunlight. Avoid harsh direct rays, especially in the afternoon, as they can scorch leaves. Low-light plants like pothos and snake plants do well in shaded corners.

Watering Tips

Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Overwatering is one of the most common mistakes. Always check the soil before adding more water. Ensure your pot has drainage holes to prevent root rot. Tropical plants may enjoy a light misting, too!

Soil & Potting

Use well-draining soil tailored to your plant’s needs. Cacti and succulents prefer sandy, gritty mixes, while leafy tropical plants do best in organic-rich potting soils. Repot every 1–2 years to refresh nutrients and give roots room to grow.

Temperature & Humidity

Keep plants in a stable, warm environment. Most houseplants prefer temperatures between 65–75°F (18–24°C). Avoid cold drafts and sudden changes. If your home is dry, especially in winter, boost humidity with a pebble tray or humidifier.

Feeding & Fertilizing

Feed monthly during the growing season (spring and summer). Use a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer for most plants. Cut back feeding in fall and winter when growth slows down. Always follow package instructions to avoid over-fertilizing.
